Course - B.Sc- IT

Like all other undergraduate programmes related to Information Technology, BSc (Bachelor of Science) in IT is basically about storing, processing, securing, and managing information. This degree is primarily focused on subjects such as software, databases, and networking. The BSc degree in IT is awarded for completing a programme of study in the field of software development, software testing, software engineering, web design, databases, programming, computer networking and computer systems. Graduates with an information technology background are able to perform technology tasks relating to the processing, storing, and communication of information between computers, mobile phones, and other electronic devices.

 

BSc IT: Eligibility Criteria

  • Candidates must have passed their 10+2 level of education from a recognised educational Board.
  • They must have Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as the main subjects, and scored a minimum of 50% marks.

Skills required

The very first step for candidates aspiring a career after completing BSc IT is to learn every detail of the key concepts of Information Technology. Major skills required for a successful career as an IT professional include the following:

Analytical skills

Problem-solving skills

Creativity

Critical-thinking skills

Resilience

 

FAQs related to Information Technology

Q. What is the meaning of BSc IT?

A. The full form of BSc IT is a Bachelor of Science in Information Technology. BSc IT is a bachelor’s degree awarded for an undergraduate course in the Information Technology field. Students seeking career opportunities in IT and software can opt for a BSc IT diploma or course. There are multiple variations available for this course in different fields and subjects, students can choose the categories according to their preferences. BSc is a gateway to work in the Information Technology industry.

Q. Which is better BSc or BTech?

A. People always get confused between BTech as only engineering and is BSc pure science course. But it is not like that. Students can opt for a BSc degree in IT. Yes, there are some differences such as the BTech has 8 semesters while BSc IT will have only 6. Some students think BTech is tough and they won’t be able to perform better in the course while others feel BSc is way too easy. 

Q. Is BSc IT a professional degree?

A. A Bachelor of IT is an undergraduate academic degree awarded after completing the course.  

Q. What is the starting salary of a BSc IT graduate?

A. BSc IT graduates can get an initial annual salary package between Rs 2.5 lakh to Rs 4 lakh per annum. Candidate with and BSc IT degree can get profiles such as programmer, system engineer, software engineer, network administrator, hardware engineer, tester, and system analyst.

Q. What should I do after BSc IT?

A. After getting a professional undergraduate degree in BSc IT, candidates either can look for opportunities in any IT company. They can also choose to complete their masters in the IT field for better salary packages and higher positions.
